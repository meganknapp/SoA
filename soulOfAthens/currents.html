Currents

Currents is an intentional community located in Glouster, Ohio that was formed in the late 1970s. They began in a multi-family house on 163-acre farmland and have since built 6 additional family homes for their members.

Organizing principle

Cooperative living has been one of the founding principles of Currents since its founding. The original members set out to live together harmoniously and to help each other our through life. Their governmental style can be categorized as democratic and egalitarian according to Laura Rose. Their governing method grew out of their experiences with the consumer co-op movement. They brought their training and experiences to Currents to build their governing system.

Homeschooling children

Throughout the community’s life, there have been new members added to the Currents family. Laura Rose joined Currents in YEAR. She has four children that she homeschools. “It’s important in order for them to have hope to overcome these huge difficulties we’re facing in the world. That’s the first part of their lives that have to be filled with happiness and getting to know the land, loving the land, seeing how food is grown and learning real skills,” Laura remarks about raising children on Currents property. Besides academic schoolwork, Laura’s children also have chores helping with the animals and plants. She also takes them on adventures through the forest that surrounds the area and teaches them how to tap maple syrup when it is in season during a fun afternoon hike.

Gathering for brunch

_____ are a family of three generations that joined Currents in 201_. ___ is still in high school while ___ is getting her associates degree at Hocking College. They all often join the other members of Currents every Sunday for brunch in the community house.

Sunday brunch is a chance for members to catch up with one another about their weeks, cozy up by a nice fire in the winter months and eat delicious homemade brunch food. This has been a tradition for the community as a way to really connect as a group over the years. After they finish eating, each member has the responsibility to wash their dishes and are encouraged to stay and talk for a while before going about their day. Conversations can vary from the kids’ schooling to future plans for the day or coming weeks or even a little bit of politics or current news issues.
