Black Locust
Collective

Black Locust Collective is a fledgling intentional community which recently purchased a 104-acre tract of land in Meigs County near Albany, Ohio. Like many millennial beginnings, Ross Martin, Kurt Belser and Sasha Segetic met online through an anarchy and racial justice list-serve. Although 20 people originally expressed interest in starting a community, which was dubbed the anarchists permaculture utopia, when it finally became a reality, only these three friends remained.

Sasha explains, “At the heart of it is the utopian hope that humans can live together and want to love each other enough to keep each other alive, without someone in charge, telling us how we should do that.” In the 12 years that have transpired, a child was born and another member, Badger Johnson, joined the group.

Black Locust’s organizing principles

Charlene Suggs, a contracts expert from the Wisteria community group is helping them draw up their land agreement. Charlene pointed out that of the 100 communities that intend to form, 99% fail. Part of being intentional about forming a community is getting agreements about how land ownership will be managed, what the guiding principal of a community will be, and how decisions will be made. Having these legal agreements is the insurance that, whatever challenges the community faces in the future, they have the means to solve.
